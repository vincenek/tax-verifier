Payment Link Verifier — Privacy & Security

Last updated: December 2025

Payment Link Verifier (PLV) is built to be privacy-first. By default, checks run entirely in your browser — URLs and derived metadata remain on your device and are not transmitted to our servers.

What we collect

PLV collects no personal identifiers by default. If you opt into usage analytics in the app Settings → Usage & Analytics, only minimal, anonymous events (action name, timestamp, basic counts) are stored locally and may be sent to an endpoint you control or configure. We never collect raw payment amounts, user names, or full financial records.

How data is handled

  • Default mode: entirely client-side, no network calls made by PLV.
  • Opt-in analytics: events are stored locally; you choose an endpoint to receive them. You can export or clear events anytime from the app.
  • Self-hosting and enterprise: you can self-host the verifier or run the batch processor locally for full control.

Transparency & open source

The codebase is public on GitHub. You can inspect the heuristics, run builds locally, or audit the exact behavior. Contributions and security reports are welcome — please open an issue on the repository.

Contact & data requests

For questions, security disclosures, or to request deletion of analytics data from your configured endpoint, contact: security@tax-verifier.example.
