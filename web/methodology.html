Methodology — How Payment Link Verifier makes decisions

PLV is designed as a fast, client-side triage tool to help teams and operators quickly identify potentially risky payment links. It intentionally focuses on lightweight, explainable signals that can be evaluated without contacting third-party services.

Primary signals

  • Protocol & host: non-HTTPS links and unusual hostnames increase risk weighting.
  • Keyword heuristics: tokens like "payee-", "verify-", "invoice-now", "update" suggest urgency or automated prompts used by attackers.
  • Shortener detection: common URL shorteners (bit.ly, tinyurl, t.co) raise suspicion because they hide destination domains.
  • TLD & domain patterns: recently-registered domains, uncommon TLDs, and lookalike domains (character swaps) are penalized.
  • Web3 address extraction: PLV detects on-chain addresses embedded in URLs and surfaces an explorer link for manual verification.

Scoring & thresholds

Each signal contributes to a simple score aggregated into a 0–100 range. The UI surfaces a human-friendly verdict (Safe vs Risky) and the numeric score. Thresholds are intentionally conservative to reduce false negatives.

False positives & human review

PLV is a first-stage triage. We recommend integrating PLV results into a workflow where suspicious items are routed for manual review before any financial action is taken.

Open & auditable

The heuristics are intentionally simple and auditable. You can run the project locally, inspect the code, or adapt rules for your organisation. For enterprise environments, we recommend self-hosting any aggregation endpoints and combining PLV with additional reputation services.
