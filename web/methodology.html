How PLV works

PLV performs client-side heuristics to highlight obvious indicators of scam or suspicious payment links. Key checks include:

  • Protocol checks: non-HTTPS or mixed content raises score.
  • Keyword heuristics: suspicious tokens like "payee-", "verify-", "invoice-now".
  • Shortener detection: common shorteners (bit.ly, tinyurl) increase risk weighting.
  • TLD and domain heuristics: uncommon or recently-registered TLDs may increase risk.
  • Web3 address extraction: detects on-chain addresses in the URL and surfaces a link to an explorer.

Limitations & false positives

These heuristics are intentionally conservative and are meant for triage. They are not a definitive blocklist and may produce false positives. Use PLV as a quick aid alongside manual checks.

Open source & transparency

The code is open source; you can inspect the checks in the repository. If you need enterprise-grade guarantees, consider integrating PLV as a first-stage triage and routing higher-risk items to manual review.
